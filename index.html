Projects

  • RFID and Vision-Based Navigation and Tracking (Recent)

    •   

    Due to the poor performance of optical cameras in occlusion, low-light and low-resolution situations, Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) is introduced for mutual complementary advantages. Phased-array antennas are employed to measure the Angle-of-Attack (AoA) of the tag, which is matched with the visual recognition results and acted as the input of RNN in order to predict the target movement.

    Currently we are researching on the selectivity of RFID to vision, with the later plan of extending to multi-target tracking.

  • Humanoid Soccer Robot

    •   

    This project aims to develop a series of soccer robots that own similar body structure to humans and are able to walk steadily, recognize and kick the ball, follow the rules of the game, etc. Motion control is the major part of my involvement, where the Zero Moment Point (ZMP) is controlled to maintain balance while walking, and specific actions are carried out through key-frame interpolation.

  • Visual Tracking

    •   

    This visual tracking system serves RoboMaster competition. The main task of my team is to identify the number on the armors and track the targets no matter where they move. Support Vector Machine (SVM) works quite well on number classification, and Kalman Filter is used to smooth the control signal of the rotation angle.

  • Robot Receptionist

    • This robot is designed to welcome and guide guests, equipped with functions such as navigation, avoidance, face recognition, voice interaction, etc. PC acts as the main controller which a mobile platform, a Kinect V1 camera and a 2D lidar are connected to. Several open source APIs and libraries (Gmapping, RGB-D SLAM, Face++, iFLYTEK) contribute to the realization of human-robot interaction.

  • Defect Detection

    • Here are four groups of pictures, among each of which is raw image, reconstructed image and result image from left to right.

    In this project, a small number of positive samples are used to train an AI model of defect detection, applying to both simple or complex background. Unsupervised learning methods such as AutoEncoder (AE) and MemAE are taken to train a network on reconstruction, together with some algorithms on morphology and feature matching which help improve the result.
